# Style DNA

## One Sentence

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, strange, product-sketch-like, visual-first with a few essential anchor labels, structurally clear through metaphor rather than instructional text.

It should feel like a product/design/AI builder quickly explaining an idea on a clean white sheet of paper.

## Required

- 16:9 horizontal body illustration for an English article.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or retro paper feel.
- Black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slightly wobbly, not mechanical, not vector-clean, not heavy outlines.
- Lots of white space: the main subject should occupy about 40%-60% of the canvas, with at least 35% quiet blank space.
- Annotation-light but not annotation-free: the metaphor should carry most of the meaning. Use 1-3 handwritten English anchor labels when they clarify the central abstraction, each ideally 1-3 words.
- One image expresses one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- The structure should be implied naturally. Do not write the structure type on the image.

## Color

- Black: main line art, Solid's hexagon body, frames, structure, primary labels, and objects.
- Red: emphasis, problem points, emotional friction, warnings, or results.
- Orange: main flow, path, arrows, automation direction, or movement from A to B.
- Blue: secondary notes, mental state, system state, feedback, assistant/AI hints, or second-layer explanation.

Blue is optional. Use color sparingly. Less is usually better.

## Absolutely Avoid

- Commercial illustration.
- PPT infographic.
- Formal flowchart.
- Course slide.
- Cute cartoon poster.
- Children's illustration.
- Complex architecture diagram.
- Polished flat illustration.
- Futuristic or tech UI aesthetic.
- Real app screenshots.
- Complex background, gradients, shadows, or texture.
- Explaining every node.
- Labels doing all the explanatory work that the metaphor should do.
- Top-left category titles such as "Workflow", "System Map", "Common Mistakes", or "Roadmap".

## Aesthetic Direction

Strange, inventive, memorable, clean, concise, and imaginative.

Not cute, childish, overloaded, or stiff.
